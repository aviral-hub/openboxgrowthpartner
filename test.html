Module 1: Requirements Engineering & Prioritization

BRD vs. FRD: The Business Requirements Document (BRD) details high-level business drivers and targeted targets (the What), while the Functional Requirements Document (FRD) clarifies software interactions and granular mechanics (the How).

MoSCoW Matrix: Core categorization criteria to optimize system scoping parameters:
• Must-Have: Vital properties. Release collapses completely without them.
• Should-Have: Important items that possess temporary manual workarounds.
• Could-Have: Discretionary upgrades implemented if runtime bandwidth parameters allow.
• Won't-Have: Fully out-of-scope for the active sprint milestone iteration.

Module 2: Visual Modeling & Process Architecture

Context Diagram: Structural scoping boundary chart representing the comprehensive core system platform as a singular bubble interaction point bounded by outside actors and incoming data fields.

Process Swimlanes: Functional lanes splitting vertical or horizontal swimlane charts. They explicitly denote who (which operational role, department, or module) owns and runs that exact task node.

RACI Matrix: Maps task ownership: Responsible (executes assignment), Accountable (has final sign-off authority; exactly one per task), Consulted (SMEs providing inputs), and Informed (notified upon delivery).

Module 3: Agile Scrum Methodology & Requirements Writing

Scrum Architecture: The Product Owner holds absolute accountability over the backlogs. The Sprint Retrospective is positioned at the terminal boundary of sprints to optimize development workflows. The Definition of Ready (DoR) defines the acceptance standard before pulling cards into active sprints.

User Story Formulation Blueprint: Standard non-technical requirement framework sequence:
As a [User Role], I want [Feature Requirement] so that [Business Value].

Module 4: Strategy Frameworks & Validation Testing

PESTLE Frame: Macro environmental audit evaluating external business constraints: Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal, and Environmental variables.

Gap Analysis & Continuous Optimization: Benchmarking operations against a desired future state structure. Lean Six Sigma focuses heavily on eliminating process waste using iterative Kaizen cycles.

Validation Engineering: User Acceptance Testing (UAT) acts as the final gate check to ensure built systems fulfill business requirements. The Requirements Traceability Matrix (RTM) maps every business criteria item to specific automated test components to eliminate scope creep.
